## Peer-Reviewed Preprints

### 1. Self-Validating Systems: A Conceptual Framework for Convergence Governance

**Abstract:** Introduces a high-level conceptual framework describing how iterative transformation processes evolve through convergence dynamics. The framework formalizes semantic transformation as the evolution of coherence under improvement, drift, and regime transitions.

- **DOI:** [10.5281/zenodo.17616682](https://zenodo.org/records/17616682)
- **Status:** Published preprint
- **Focus:** Conceptual framework, governance architecture

### 2. Semantic Thermodynamics: A Formal Model for Coherence Dynamics under Drift

**Abstract:** Formalizes semantic transformation as the evolution of coherence under improvement, drift, and regime transitions. Introduces the semantic Hamiltonian:

```
H(s) = αG(s) + βI(s) + γU(s) - δEv(s)
```

The framework shows that coherence loss follows entropy-like dynamics, and that self-validation naturally emerges when a system minimizes semantic energy under continuous drift.

- **DOI:** [10.5281/zenodo.17618208](https://zenodo.org/records/17618208)
- **Status:** Published preprint
- **Focus:** Formal model, mathematical foundations

---

### 3. Empirical Thermodynamic Convergence in Semantic State Spaces

**Abstract:** First empirical evaluation of semantic thermodynamic dynamics derived from the SYNTRIAD framework. Demonstrates:

1. Bounded semantic energy under controlled drift
2. Exponential convergence under corrective forces
3. Emergence of equilibrium distributions consistent with Gibbs dynamics
4. Automatic detection of inconsistencies (self-falsification behavior)

- **DOI:** [10.5281/zenodo.17618998](https://zenodo.org/records/17618998)
- **Status:** Published preprint
- **Focus:** Empirical validation, experimental results
